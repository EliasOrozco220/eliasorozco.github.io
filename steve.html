Steveveharvevey

2026

(February 10, 2026)

This is a glitched photo of Steve Harvey, a beloved African-American comedian and show host. He is shown here being tripled into three of himself and if you look even closer, you can see it's almost at a loop of continuous Steve Harveys. The original photo depicts of Mr. Harvey with his classic black suit and a complimentary brown tie and a white shirt. In this glitched form, he has only black and white colors.

I have become interested in my subject choice because I have glitched an astronaut the last time, but not a person who you can actually see their face on, plus it's Steve Harvey. I used Adobe Photoshop to save a copy of the original photo as a "Photoshop Raw". From there, I used the "Audacity" application to finally glitch the photo. I first had to download these two applications through google. Through "Audacity", I imported my raw data file of the big H and it showed me a sound-wave. I thought it was pretty cool to actually see that since you technically can't hear a picture. So from there, I tampered with the audio by adding sound effects such as reverb, adjusting the reverb settings as well. After being done with that, I had saw that the sound-wave looked a little different and then I exported that file and put it back on my computer. I viewed the same photo again on adobe and it had changed into this finale photo.

Now why Steve Harvey? At first, I did try out some other photos (a lot of batman ones) and it didn't work how I expected them to. Sure they were glitched, but I could barely make out what the final photo was, it looked way too distorted and static-y to the point where the photos didn't look nothing short of strange black and white streaks. So then I looked to Harvey. The photo had a clear white background and the Harvey himself was directly centered in it with bold colors extracting that blankness with some darker color. What I also appreciated was that Harvey didn't have too much different colors in the photo so it wouldn't look too off when glitched. I found him to be the perfect subject to actually study how color works in a photo and how it's more visually distinct when there's not too many colors in a photo, in other words, it's not being complicated and sticks to a simple color theme while still knowing it's Steve Harvey.
