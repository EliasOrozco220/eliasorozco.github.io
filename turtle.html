All Quiet on the Ocean Front

(March 23, 2026)

My work is called "All Quiet on the Ocean Front" as a nod to the war book and film that hold the same title but it's "Western Front" instead. It is showing the intense mission little baby sea turtles have to go on right after being hatched from their eggs. The enemy or danger in this process are birds, more specifically, seagulls. As a lot of birds do actually eat baby sea turtles since they are tiny and easy to eat while they cross the sandy beach to finally find life in the ocean. There's cuts of old war movies to compare in intensity and to exaggerate the process.

I'm already into turtles as whole. I used to have tiny sea turtles when I was little, I forgot what happened to them... I do have a pet tortoise that I've had for 6 years now, his names Gus. Anyways I wanted to make them my subject for the video process. Also, I'm a fool for animal wildlife survival documentaries so I looked into two different sea turtle ones since I already had a vision of what I wanted to do. I found all the source media on google, it is two different documents on sea turtles crossing to get to the ocean while dodging birds, short scenes from war films, and the iconic song from the movie "The Good, the Bad, and the Ugly", titled "Ecstasy of gold". It's a very recognizable soundtrack. One of the documents was a National Geographic which are pretty much the ones you'd watch if you wann learn about animals, it's reliable. And the beginning has the opening scene from "Saving Private Ryan". I used Premiere to make this. To do all this, I cut a ton of video footage and clip it on other parts. I extracted the audio from all the videos and finally added audio in the background.

I made this to show the intense start to a sea turtle's life. It's funny how they know exactly where to go when leaving the egg. The portrayal is really to show how dangerous an animals life can be at some point. In this case, it's for baby sea turtles. I want the viewers to see this and get a silly feeling from it but also take into consideration the survival instincts some animals have. I would also like it to show how even those baby turtles have a sort of personality while they see the video.
